However, Fibbage is based on a social game platform, so the social interactions and goals are also important to consider in this game design. Therefore, I added other breadths to the jouney map, including social behaviors and social goals. The new journey map also uncovers some potential opportunities, which could implicate future design.

Design Process

I started by sketching out a few key screens based on the user flow identified, and created a more detailed wireframe. While laying out the design, I came across three challenges.

Challenges

1. Horizontal versus vertical layout
It was a challenge to put everything on the screen: the controls, the text contents, and up to 6 avatars. I started to consider using a horizontal layout, with the contents in the center and avatars lining up on the left and right edge of the screen. This looks like a good plan at first, but the game has a huge social interaction need-the player might want to text chat with the fellow players throughout the game. This means the keyboard will always pop up, taking a huge portion of the screen; and most users are not used to a horizontal keyboard. Therefore, I decided to still go with the vertical layout.

↑ Low-fidelity Design

2. Showing all avatars on the screen
One of the requirement for the design is that "User avatars must be visible on-screen in the game". However, with the vertical layout and up to six players, it is hard to find a place to put everything without making the avatars small or close together. After trying out different methods, I was inspired by the new iOS group Facetime design-only active users are shown at the center of the screen. In my design, I made the bottom section big enough to show four avatars on the surface, but all the avatars could be accessed through a simple sliding gesture. Also, when an avatar speaks or acts, he/she will be moved to the front to be visible.


3. Text input confusion (Mode error)
Due to heavy social interaction in the game, it is highly possible that the player might confuse the answer input box with the chat input box, and accidently send the answer to all the players. Therefore, I designed a flow where users can only enter a separate mode to enter the answer.

Design Guidelines

The color scheme, typography and components are decided based and the main app and following the iOS 11 Design Guidelines. UI Elements are made consistent with the main app, but I also added a bright blue to the primary color in order to make the UI more lively inside the game.

Reflection

The Fibbage game design still has a lot to improve. The next step for me would be to make a higher fidelity interactive prototype and carry out a user testing to validate the design and improve usability. Also, some of the opportunities identified in the journey map are not yet reflected in the final design, and would be the focus of next round's design.

The design concept could be applied to other strategy/word games. An interesting idea would be the game of Monoply, because the capbility of the avatars will make the game experience more interactive and a lot of fun.
